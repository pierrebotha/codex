# Story Bible: Between Two Worlds



This document provides the mandatory grounding for all subagents working on the expansion of *Between Two Worlds*.



## 1. Stylistic Constraints (The Unmistakable Fingerprint)

- **British English**: Use British spelling (honour, colour, centre, realise, etc.) and grammar.

- **Dialogue**: Use **single quotation marks** ('Like this') for all speech.

- **Punctuation**: 

    - **NO em dashes (    )**. Use colons, commas, or full stops for breaks.

    - **NO tricola**. Avoid groups of three (e.g., "the wind, the sea, and the rain"). Use pairs or single descriptors.

- **Sensory Grounding**: Every scene must have a physical, sensory anchor (smell of diesel, vibration of the tram, salt on the skin).



## 2. Mandatory Thematic Vocabulary

Every chapter expansion should integrate at least three of these phrases where appropriate:

*   **Mechanical, Bored Precision**: Used for the bureaucracy, the state, or legal machinery.

*   **Stable Geography**: Used for institutional permanence or the illusion of safety.

*   **Dark Grammar**: Used for the emerging laws of apartheid and exclusion.

*   **Nervous Constitution**: Used to describe the psychological fragility of characters caught between worlds.

*   **Atmospheric Pressure**: The social weight of exclusion or expectation.

*   **Bureaucratic Theatre**: The performance of order in state offices.

*   **Ghoema Beat**: The suppressed but resilient rhythm of the Cape culture.



## 3. Core Character Manifest



| Character | Role | Key Traits |

| :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Ruth MacLeod** | Protagonist | Scholarship girl, farm-bred, "Truly between two worlds," intellectual as a survival strategy. |

| **Judith Cohen** | Best Friend | Daughter ofwealthy Jewish family, vibrant, defiant, navigates the "Double Life" with ease. |

| **Sarah** | Domestic Worker | Kaaps-speaking, sharp wit, the first bridge to the "Secret of the District." |

| **Professor Arbuthnot** | Mentor/Warden | Classicist, robes smelling of tobacco, teaches the "Logic of the Dead." |

| **Clara** | Love Interest | Independent, short-haired, political, lives in the Gardens flat; a "Door Left Ajar." |

| **Thomas Lawrence** | Uncle | War veteran, "Damaged Masculinity," managing a "Geography of Silence." |

| **Oom Piet** | Relative | Representing the farm's failure and the "Voice of the Veld." |



## 4. Narrative Arc

The story follows Ruth's transition from a naive scholarship student to a politically awakened witness. The "Unmaking" of her academic career by the state (due to her associations) is her catalyst for finding a true, unpartitioned voice.
